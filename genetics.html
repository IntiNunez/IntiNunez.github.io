Genetic Diversity Lab

For this experiment, we took samples of cheek cells to seperate our DNA with DNA extraction, then we expanded a specific section of our DNA, called the D1S80 VNTR, into billions of copies with PCR amplification, then we compared our DNA to our classmates using the length of the strands through gel electrophoresis to discover the human diversity in our classroom. Before we started the experiment, we conducted an interview within our table group to make predictions on what results we would get.

Preparing the DNA

This step is split up into two phases, the DNA Extraction then the PCR Amplificiation phase, first we first took a toothpick to collect our cheek cells, storing it in an a tube with buffer, then we had to heat it up, which destroys the rest of the cells and protiens to prepare it for the PCR Amplification phase. Then we are ready for our next step. To do this, we need to first needed to prepare a new tube with ingredients such as, a master mix and a primer for our VNTR section. Once we had our tube prepared we were now able to place our small sample of DNA, only two micro liters, in the tube, mixing it with the other ingredients. Then we placed the mix in the miniPCR machine and started doubling the size of the DNA, from one sample to billions.

Measuring and Comparing

After the DNA was ready, we put all of the samples into a gel block, then we ran electricity through the gel, which after an hour, dragged the DNA to its length. After the process was complete we measured the distances using the number of pixels of a photo and using a complicated equation to get a rough estimate on the number of allele bases each strand has. Then we put the results into a spreadsheet to review and discuss. The expected result, which was that where you came from affected who you would have a match with, was not supported. We then took a post interview to discuss the results and created a personal CER to share what we thought about race.

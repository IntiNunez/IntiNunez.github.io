Inti's Mystery Black Box Investigation and Project

In my freshman year at Engineering and Science University Magnet School, I was challenged in my New Media and Technology class on what is in a secret Black Box. When we poured water into a funnel that was at the top, different amounts of water came out. I used multiple platforms including Adobe Photoshop and Autodesk Maya to try to decipher what was inside the black box.

Claim

My theory was that if the water was put into the box at different speeds a different amount of water would come out. I thought that if the water went slower or with less pressure from behind it would enter one hole and the amount of water that comes out would be the same, but if it had more speed or pressure, the water would pass the first hole and enter a storage container to get stored so less water would come out, but if it were even faster with more pressure, the water would exit the tube is in hit a lever that opens the storage container, and make any water that was previously stored be let out.

Evidence and Reasoning

When I was testing on the black box I inserted different amounts of water then measured how much came out. We used graduated cylinders to insert different amounts of water then once another tube came out in the bottom, we measured it again to gather the data. We were testing on the original black box to gather more information to generate our theory. While I was trying to prove my theory I picked out some data sets. For example in trial #9, we poured in 100 mL of water and 1940 mL of water came out. This might be because the running total of water stored the trial before was at a high of 1980 mL. In trial 24-25 the stored water went from 598 mL to 298 mL which means that the amount of water stored does not affect when it comes out. This supports my theory because in my theory, when the water is released depends on the speed on the water needed so that it could push a lever.

While constructing our representation of the black box, we were tasked with taking photos to track our progress. This went from taking photos of the box itself to us working on the design. There were some strategies we used to capture better pictures including the rule of thirds, different framings, and different angles. Each one of these strategies help capture what we were trying to display whether it was a specific task or the entire box.

2D Design and 3D Recreation and Modeling

When creating our designs on what we thought was in the black box, we used Adobe Photoshop to help our ideas be expressed through their drawing tools. We used various brush types and tools to make it better show the different parts of our design. We had to keep in mind what materials we had available when creating our black box including,

  • Cardboard box
  • Plastic Tubes
  • Funnel
  • Buckets
  • Tape
  • Glue
  • And More

The design steps went from our initial thoughts to animating the design to show how we thought the water would flow in the box. This process helped us to move onto the actual building phase of our own black boxes.

Once we created our actual box, we designed it in Autodesk Maya to help show our box in the virtual world. In the beginning of this process we simply created our basic components with modeling and then eventually moved on to more complex parts of our box. After we modeled our box, we animated it to show how the box would work from opening the box’s doors to the camera’s movements. We rigged a hand to either point out something in the box, or simply welcome the viewer with a hand wave. Once we fully finished our box and modeling, we added lights and changed our box’s materials and the parts. Once we had fully modeled, rigged, animated, and customized materials, we fully rendered in Maya’s “Arnold” or “Pretty” mode which gave the box a more realistic look than needed when just simply modeling.

Skill Reflection

Throughout this project, we had to communicate with our team members, so we could complete the box with a more fluid and productive process. We could not have completed our box without this skill as we all needed to be working on different parts of the box at the same time to fit the deadline. This helped to make sure we were successfully contributing to this project and to help no one feel like they didn’t know what they were supposed to do, or what the group was doing. One example of this is that before we got started on our box, we talked and saw what we each had to do and what we were going to do with the help of a board where we kept track of our tasks and where they were in the process of being completed.

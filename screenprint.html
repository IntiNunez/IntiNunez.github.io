Inti's Screenprinting Art Project

In this project, we were assigned the task of creating a screenprint on a piece of fabric about something that we wanted to share with others. For my project, I decided to create a piece that would inspire people to chase their dreams.

Brainstorming and Planning

To start off my project, I had to create a first draft in Adobe Photoshop of what
I wanted my screenprint to be about. I decided to have me reaching up into the
skies. Then, I got help to take a picture of me acting like I was pointing up
into the skies, so I stood on a stepladder so that I could act like I was stepping
on the clouds, then I had to edit out the background, condense the small little
pixels into bigger sections, and then change the color to a solid black.
Now I could create a second draft with my selected text, the first stanza of
Dreams by Langston Hughes, graphics, like the clouds I was stepping on and the butterfly
that is on my finger, then I decided my two colors, black for the base, my body,
the clouds, and other, and an orange for the buttefly to make it look like a Monarch.
Then I had to use Adobe Illustrator to calculate the dimensions of the materials
such as the wood frame, mesh, and vinyl. I also used Illustrator's color picker
to find out what combonation of colors I would need to use from the
CMYK (Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, and Black) color model to obtain the orange I had picked earlier in Photoshop.

Building and Printing

I started by cutting the wood pieces to size and stapling the mesh screen tightly onto the frame. After that, I ran my design through the vinyl cutter and used an iron to heat-press the vinyl stencils onto the mesh. Since my design had two colors, I had to make two separate frames—one for the black ink and one for the orange. Once the screens were prepped, I lined them up on the fabric and used a squeegee to print the ink through the mesh. After everyone finished their individual prints, we sewed all the fabric pieces together into a class display.
